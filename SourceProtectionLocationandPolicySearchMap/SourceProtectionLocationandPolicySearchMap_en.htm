Source Protection Interactive Mapping Tool (SPIMT)

Under the source protection program, nineteen source protection committees worked with conservation authorities and municipalities across Ontario to assess the vulnerability of sources of municipal drinking water. These assessments were documented in 38 local assessment reports that were approved by the ministry. The data collected from these reports forms the foundation for the Source Protection Interactive Mapping Tool.

This interactive map allows you to search for a location in Ontario and determine if it is in a Significant Threat Source Protection vulnerable area. The map will return the location information, vulnerable zone type and score, as well as provide link(s) to groundwater and surface water policies associated with the search location.

Searches can be performed by street address, assessment roll number, geographic coordinates or several other search options listed in the help. Please note that searches by roll number and coordinates will return the most accurate results. Searches performed with a street address, postal code, municipality, lot, concession or township will require you to further refine the location by moving the pin to the accurate location because of the inaccuracy of these spatial references. Please refer to the help for tips on searching and the proper syntax of your searches.

The Source Protection Assessment Reports and Plans and the Risk Measures Catalogue are also available directly from Conservation Ontario.

To obtain a password for the policy database or for additional help, please contact source.protection@ontario.ca.
